Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

General FAQs

How is Starbucks Reserve different from Starbucks?

Starbucks Reserve is the most premium and immersive expression of Starbucks. These flagship locations around the world showcase rare, small‑lot coffees and offer experiences you won’t find in a typical Starbucks coffeehouse. Guests can explore exceptional single‑origin coffees, craft beverages, and cocktails such as the Signature Espresso Martini, along with fresh, elevated food and exclusive merchandise. Many locations also offer interactive coffee bars, tastings, and workshops that highlight coffee craft. Each Starbucks Reserve destination is uniquely designed to celebrate innovation, artistry, and local culture, creating a one‑of‑a‑kind experience in every city.

What's the difference between a Starbucks Reserve Roastery and Starbucks Reserve coffeehouse/bar?

Roasteries are large, flagship destinations featuring coffee roasting and mixology bars. Reserve Bars are smaller, offering Starbucks Reserve coffees and elevated brewing methods.

How many Starbucks Reserve locations are there?

There are currently five Starbucks Reserve Roasteries worldwide, located in Chicago, New York City, Milan, Shanghai, and Tokyo. In addition, there is a Starbucks Reserve coffeehouse in New York City’s Empire State Building, as well as Starbucks Reserve Bars and coffeehouses in select cities around the globe.

Do I need to make a reservation?

Reservations are not required for entry, but they are recommended for special experiences like coffee tastings or mixology classes.

Are Starbucks Reserve locations animal-friendly?

Due to food safety laws, only ADA-recognized service dogs and specifically trained miniature horses (not Emotional Support Animals) are allowed inside Starbucks Reserve locations.

Stores accept the local currency. Starbucks app or gift card balances must be in USD—other currencies aren’t accepted.

Merchandise & Whole Bean Coffee

Do all Starbucks Reserve locations offer merchandise and whole bean coffee?

Yes, each Starbucks Reserve location offers exclusive merchandise and whole bean coffee.

Can I get Starbucks Reserve merchandise and whole bean coffee at a Starbucks café?

No, Starbucks Reserve merchandise and whole bean coffee are only available at Starbucks Reserve locations.

Do you ship merchandise to me?

No, shipping is not available at this time.

What is your merchandise or whole bean coffee return policy?

Merchandise or packaged whole bean coffee may be returned within 60 days of purchase with the original receipt. Refunds will be issued to the original form of payment, except for purchases made using PayPal or a Starbucks Card, which will be refunded in the form of Starbucks Store Credit.

How long is Starbucks Reserve whole bean coffee good for?

Freshness varies depending on how the coffee is packaged and stored:

Unopened sealed bags of Starbucks Reserve whole bean coffee stay fresh for up to 12 months. Refer to the "Best Before" date printed on the bag.

Starbucks Reserve Whiskey Barrel‑Aged Guatemala has a shorter freshness window. Because its freshness date is tracked by lot number, the best way to confirm its ideal use‑by date is to ask your barista when purchasing.

Freshly scooped whole bean coffee is best enjoyed up to 14 days after the “Roasted On” date handwritten on the bag.

Once opened, we recommend enjoying your coffee as soon as possible and storing it in an airtight container, away from heat and light, to preserve its best flavor.

What’s the difference between scooped bags and sealed bags of Starbucks Reserve whole bean coffee?

Starbucks Reserve whole bean coffee is offered in two formats, each designed for a different type of coffee experience. Sealed 250‑gram whole bean bags include a one‑way Flavor Lock™ valve that helps preserve freshness, giving the coffee an unopened shelf life of up to 12 months. Hand‑scooped whole bean bags are freshly portioned in-store and best enjoyed up to 14 days after the “Roasted On” date handwritten on the bag. For optimal flavor, we recommend storing all coffee in an airtight container away from heat and light once opened.

If I purchase whole bean coffee at a Starbucks Reserve location, can my neighborhood Starbucks coffeehouse grind it for me?

Yes. Any U.S. Starbucks coffeehouse can grind Starbucks Reserve whole bean coffee for you.

Is Starbucks Reserve merchandise different from what I can find at my local Starbucks?

Yes. Merchandise is uniquely designed and curated for Starbucks Reserve locations, offering specialty items that highlight the elevated coffee experience and the distinct character of each location.

Starbucks Rewards & Starbucks Gift Cards

Can I redeem Stars at Starbucks Reserve locations?

Yes. You can redeem Stars at U.S. Starbucks Reserve locations on eligible items. Redemption options may vary by location. Alcohol purchases are excluded.

Can I redeem Stars for alcohol at Starbucks Reserve locations?

No. Alcohol cannot be redeemed with Stars. Local laws and regulations prohibit offering discounts or incentives on alcoholic beverages, so alcohol is excluded from Starbucks Rewards redemptions.

What can I redeem my Stars for at U.S. Starbucks Reserve locations?

At U.S. Starbucks Reserve locations, you can redeem Stars for eligible items such as handcrafted beverages, merchandise, whole bean coffee, coffee flights, and select experiences. Redemption values and availability vary by location. Click here to view the full list of Starbucks Rewards redemption options.

Can I earn Stars at Starbucks Reserve locations?

Yes. You can earn Stars at U.S. Starbucks Reserve locations on eligible purchases. Alcohol purchases are excluded.

Can I earn Stars when I purchase alcohol at Starbucks Reserve locations?

No. Alcohol purchases cannot earn Stars. Local laws and regulations prohibit offering incentives on alcoholic beverages.

How do I earn Stars at U.S. Starbucks Reserve locations?

You can earn Stars on eligible purchases at U.S. Starbucks Reserve locations when you pay with your registered Starbucks Card or the Starbucks app. Earning rates follow the same structure used at Starbucks coffeehouses. Alcohol purchases and select items are excluded. Click here to learn more about earning Stars and to view current earning levels.

Can I use Starbucks gift cards at Starbucks Reserve locations?

Yes. Starbucks gift cards are accepted at U.S. Starbucks Reserve locations. International acceptance may vary.

Can I purchase Starbucks Reserve gift cards at Starbucks Reserve locations?

Yes, Starbucks gift cards can be purchased at U.S. Starbucks Reserve locations.

Can Starbucks Reserve gift cards be used at Starbucks coffeehouses?

Yes, Starbucks Reserve gift cards can be used at U.S. Starbucks coffeehouses..

Can I redeem my Starbucks Birthday Reward at Starbucks Reserve locations?

Yes, Starbucks Rewards members can redeem their Birthday Reward at U.S. Starbucks Reserve locations.

Can I redeem Stars for Starbucks Reserve merchandise or whole bean coffee?

Yes. Starbucks Rewards members can redeem Stars for Starbucks Reserve merchandise, whole bean coffee, coffee flights, or select experiences valued up to $20. If the total exceeds $20, members can pay the difference. Redemption options vary by location.

Does Starbucks Reserve offer Mobile Order & Pay?

Mobile Order & Pay is not available at U.S. Starbucks Reserve Roasteries and may be limited or unavailable at other Starbucks Reserve locations.
